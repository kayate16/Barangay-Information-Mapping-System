About the System

Barangay Information Mapping System for Cagpile, Oras, Eastern Samar

Project Overview

This Barangay Information Mapping System is developed as part of the research study conducted by Eastern Samar State University. The system leverages QGIS and modern web technologies to provide an accessible platform for community planning and disaster risk reduction.

Our mission is to digitize barangay information for better decision-making, disaster preparedness, and community development planning.

Background & Context

Barangay Cagpile in Oras, Eastern Samar is a coastal community exposed to natural hazards such as typhoons, storm surges, and flooding. These risks highlight the critical need for accurate spatial information for preparedness and response planning.

Traditional manual mapping methods are being replaced by this digital GIS-based solution to improve accuracy, accessibility, and decision-making capabilities for barangay officials and emergency responders.

The system represents a significant step forward in community-based disaster risk reduction and sustainable development planning.

Project Objectives

The system aims to achieve the following key objectives for Barangay Cagpile:

Data Collection

Collect comprehensive GPS and demographic data of all households and facilities in Barangay Cagpile using modern surveying techniques.

Digital Mapping

Create a comprehensive GIS-based digital map using QGIS that accurately represents the barangay's spatial layout and infrastructure.

Information Accessibility

Provide an interactive web-based platform for easy access to barangay information for officials, researchers, and community members.

Disaster Preparedness

Support disaster risk reduction through accurate spatial data, vulnerability assessment, and emergency planning capabilities.

Community Development

Enable evidence-based planning for infrastructure development, social services, and resource allocation.

Research Platform

Serve as a foundation for academic research in community informatics, disaster management, and geographic information systems.
